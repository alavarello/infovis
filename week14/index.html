The UN defines unpaid work as: “activities including food preparation, dishwashing, cleaning and upkeep of the dwelling, laundry, ironing, gardening, caring for pets, shopping, installation, servicing and repair of personal and household goods, childcare, and care of the sick, elderly, or disabled household and family members, among others.”

The UN Office of the High Commissioner for Human Rights states that:

“it is estimated that if unpaid care work were assigned a monetary value it would constitute between 10 and 39 per cent of GDP.

However, it is generally unrecognized and under-valued by policy-makers and legislators. This status quo has far-reaching negative consequences for whole nations and societies.
